About Swatch

Founded in 1983, Swatch revolutionized the watch industry with its colorful, affordable, and high-quality Swiss watches. The name "Swatch" is a contraction of "second watch", as the company positioned its products as fun, collectible accessories.

Swatch watches are known for their innovative designs, use of bold colors, and creative collaborations with artists and designers. Despite their playful appearance, all Swatch watches are precision Swiss-made timepieces.

Today, Swatch continues to push boundaries with new materials, technologies, and designs while staying true to its original mission of bringing Swiss quality to a wider audience.

Our History

Swatch was born out of the Swiss watch industry's need to respond to the quartz crisis of the 1970s and early 1980s. When Asian manufacturers flooded the market with inexpensive, accurate quartz watches, the Swiss industry was in danger of collapse.

The solution came from a combination of innovative manufacturing techniques, creative design, and a new approach to watch marketing. By reducing the number of components from the usual 91 or more to just 51, Swatch could produce high-quality watches at a fraction of the traditional cost.

The first Swatch collection launched in 1983 with 12 models. The watches were an immediate success, and by 1984, production reached one million units. Today, Swatch continues to innovate while maintaining its position as the world's best-selling watch brand.
